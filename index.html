Worm Holes:

Wormholes are theoretical tunnels that could connect two different points in space and time. They are often described as shortcuts through the universe, like a tunnel connecting two distant places instead of traveling the long distance between them. Wormholes come from solutions to Einstein's theory of general relativity, but scientists have not yet found any evidence that they actually exist.

Wormholes may form from the bending of spacetime, which is caused by massive objects like stars and planets. Some scientists think they could be related to black holes, but a black hole and a wormhole are not the same thing. A wormhole would require special conditions to remain open, possibly involving a type of matter with unusual properties that has not been discovered.

If you traveled through a wormhole, you might be able to reach a distant part of the universe much faster than normal space travel would allow. However, most theories suggest that wormholes could be unstable and might collapse before anything could pass through them. If they do exist, scientists still do not know whether they could safely transport people or objects.
